Quick Start Guide

Get up and running in minutes.

01

API Key Setup

Head to Settings. You have two ways to connect your AI Chefs:

Option A: Direct

Paste your raw API keys directly into the input fields.

Option B: Pointers

Export keys in your shell (e.g. export GEMINI_API_KEY="...") and enter the variable name in settings.

02

Load the Pantry

Update your Inventory by typing naturally: "I have 3 chicken breasts, half a bag of spinach, and some heavy cream." Arby will prioritize using these items in your next plan.

03

Build your Cookbook

Arby loves your existing recipes. Go to the Library and click Sync PDFs. The AI Librarian will read your local files and turn them into digital recipes. You can also manually add secrets from your head at any time.

04

Set the Mood

On the homepage, use the Recipe Ideas modal. Tell Arby what you're craving (e.g. "Healthy Mediterranean") or demand a specific recipe from your library. This guides the generation engine before you even click plan.

Plan & Generate

How Syncing Works

Arby monitors your PDF_FOLDER (configured in settings). When you click **Sync PDFs**, it scans for new files. If a file hasn't been processed, the **Librarian** reads every page and converts it to Arby's standard JSON format.

Troubleshooting FAQ

Why is my connection failing?

Usually, this is due to an unexported API key. Ensure you used the export keyword in your shell profile and ran ./arby restart.

How do I fix a corrupted state?

If Arby seems "stuck," you can run python3 app/scripts/init_state.py to reset your configuration files to defaults (be careful, this won't delete data but will reset settings).

My PDF won't sync!

The Librarian requires Google Gemini for PDF ingestion. Ensure your Google key is active and that the PDF is not password protected.

Where is my data stored?

All your recipes, history, and preferences are stored locally in the state/ folder. Nothing is ever sent to our servers.
